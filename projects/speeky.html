Thesis
Public speaking confidence isn’t built overnight
Speeky turns practice into a daily, approachable habit.

Speeky is a mobile app prototype designed to help users improve their public speaking skills through consistent, low-pressure practice. Instead of overwhelming users with theory, the app focuses on short daily challenges, guided speaking sessions, and AI-driven feedback to build confidence over time. The goal was to transform public speaking from something intimidating into something engaging and achievable.

Ideation

Design directions explored

Traditional learning

Initial direction focused on structured lessons and tips, but it felt overwhelming and not suited for users who avoid high-pressure situations.

Daily confidence building ✦

The final concept focuses on small, daily speaking challenges designed to gradually build confidence in a safe and private environment.

Approach

How it was built - step by step

01

User interviews

Conducted interviews with introverted students to understand their experiences with public speaking, focusing on anxiety triggers, avoidance behaviors, and preferred learning environments.

Research
02

Empathy mapping

Translated interview insights into empathy maps to better understand user thoughts, feelings, and pain points, which helped guide design decisions toward a more supportive experience.

UX Research
03

Low-fidelity prototyping

Created low-fidelity wireframes to explore user flows such as onboarding, daily challenges, and practice sessions, allowing for quick iteration and feedback.

Wireframing
04

Prototype & testing

Developed interactive Figma prototypes and recorded prototype walkthrough videos to test usability and refine the overall experience based on feedback.
