Thesis
Group decisions are slowed by coordination overhead - SplitDecision removes the friction so teams can move faster and more fairly.

Making decisions as a group is deceptively hard. Scheduling a meeting, choosing an option, or splitting into teams involves rounds of back-and-forth messages, forgotten replies, and no single source of truth. SplitDecision was built to replace that friction with one collaborative space where polls, games, and availability all live together and respond to every participant in real time.

Ideation

Design directions explored

Calendar-first

A second direction centred on scheduling alone. Useful but too narrow. It solved availability without addressing the actual decision-making moment once a time was agreed.

Unified hub ✦

The chosen direction combines polls, games (wheel, horse race, RPS), collaborative calendar, and team management in one space so any type of group decision has a home.

How it was built - step by step

Research & scoping

02

Low-fi prototyping & task flows

Sketched user flows for each feature individually, then mapped how they connected team creation gating access to games and calendar. Key insight: the calendar had to work asynchronously.

Design
03

Calendar & game implementation

The availability calendar required the most iteration. Drag-to-select across time slots needed precise mouse-event tracking with touchstart fallbacks. Consolidating overlapping availability across users meant designing a slot-shading algorithm that communicates consensus strength visually; darker shading = more people free.
The Spin the Wheel and Horse Racing games were built with CSS animations driven by JS randomness, keeping them lightweight with no external libraries.

Development
04

Testing & refinement

User testing revealed that calendar drag behaviour was unintuitive on mobile. Feedback also led to the hover-highlight system so users could preview slot overlap before committing a selection.
