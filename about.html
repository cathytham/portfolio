Biography

Cathy Tham is a student currently pursuing a bachelor’s degree in computer science and Computation Arts at Concordia University in Montreal, Quebec. Her interests revolve around interactive digital experiences that explore the way people interact with technology, particularly with web applications. She wants to create spaces that allow users to participate and make decisions.
Her projects include a various range of full-stack and AI-integrated applications, such as SplitDecision, a group decision-making website that allows users to create polls, organize teams, and manage shared schedules through a collaborative calendar. She has also developed an AI-powered recipe generator that combines image recognition and generative models to produce personalized recipes and visuals based on the user input. Across her work, she uses tools such as React, JavaScript, Python, and database systems to build interactive applications that focus collaboration, communication, and engagement.
Beside her academic work, Cathy has gained practical experience in the field through an internship in mobile application development, which involved working with React Native, Node.js, and MySQL in a SCRUM environment. She has experience in both the development and design sides of projects, which enables her to look at projects from different angles. She is currently interested in further exploring interactive systems, artificial intelligence, and digital environments that support collaboration and real-world decision-making.

More about me

Statement

My work focuses mainly on interactive digital experiences and how people interact with technology, websites, and AI systems. I am interested in how users interact with digital environments and how these systems respond, adapt or generate content based on user input. Through my projects, I have explored themes of interaction, collaboration and artificial intelligence where users are the primary participants rather than passive viewers. I primarily work with web technologies and programming languages such as JavaScript, React, Python, and database systems. My process usually starts with an idea for an interactive system, and then I design and build a website or application where the user’s actions influence the result. One example is SplitDecision, a group decision-making website where users can create polls, form teams, and share availability through a collaborative calendar. This project focuses on how people make decisions together and how a digital platform can organize and influence that process. Many of my projects focus on interaction, communication, and systems that change based on user input.
Through my work, I want to create interactive and accessible digital experiences that make technology feel more creative, collaborative, and engaging. I am interested in continuing to explore interactive environments that simulate intelligent behaviour and digital environments that reflect real-world collaboration and decision-making.

— Cathy Tham, 2026
